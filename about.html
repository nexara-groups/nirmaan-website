Principal Architect

Suresh Kumar Bandaru

Suresh Kumar Bandaru is the founding principal and chief architect of Sai Nirmaan Architects, established in 2011. With over 15 years of professional practice and 300+ projects across 4+ states, Suresh has shaped a diverse portfolio spanning Architecture, Interior, and Landscape Design throughout South India and Odisha.

Suresh holds a B.Arch from SAR College of Architecture (JNAFAU), and an M.Plan(Environmental) from Andhra University. He is registered with the Council of Architecture (COA Registered Architect), and is a Fellow of the Indian Institute of Architects (IIA Fellow), a member of the Indian Institute of Interior Designers (IIID Member), and the Indian Green Building Council (IGBC). Suresh has served as Chairman of IIID Vizag Centre (2021–2023, 2023–2025) and as EC Member of the IIA Andhra Pradesh Chapter (2022–2024). His commitment to human-centered design and community impact is evident in landmark projects including the Tribal Museum (Odisha, 2023) and the Indoor Stadium for SAAP. He leads humanitarian housing initiatives through the DRIS programme, partnering with CARE India, Child Fund, Save the Children, and ADRA to build disaster-resilient settlements in coastal Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, UP, West Bengal and Odisha.

Our Philosophy

Architecture that serves people first

Good architecture is not just about buildings — it is about creating spaces that uplift, inspire, and serve the people who use them. I believe that every project, regardless of scale, deserves thoughtful attention to context, climate, and community. My approach is rooted in empathy, sustainability, and a commitment to design that quietly improves lives.

Whether designing a family home, a civic institution, or a landscape, I strive to balance beauty and function, crafting environments that are both purposeful and enduring.

Good architecture doesn't shout — it quietly improves the life of everyone who passes through it.

— Suresh Kumar Bandaru
